## STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS

<STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS>
Design Aesthetic: Authoritative institutional banking aesthetic with HSBC Red as a precision accent on pure white canvases. Layouts are structured but breathe through generous whitespace, with section subheaders anchored by short red underline rules and data carrying the narrative weight. The overall impression is confident, global, analytically precise — never decorative.

Background:
  Texture: Pure flat white canvas with no texture, gradient, or pattern. Occasional light grey (#F5F5F5) card surfaces group related content without heavy borders. Crisp, clinical, and clean.
  Base Color: Pure White (#FFFFFF)

Typography:
  Headlines: Bold geometric sans-serif with clean modern proportions, neutral letterforms, large scale (48–60px equivalent), near-black color, left-aligned, sentence case. Univers Next Pro character — confident but not decorative.
  Body: Regular-weight clean sans-serif matching the headline family, medium size (16–18px), dark grey, generous line-height. Section subheaders are bold with a short HSBC Red underline rule (~40–60px wide) directly beneath.

Color Palette:
  Primary Text: Near Black (#1A1A1A) - slide titles and primary headlines
  Background: Pure White (#FFFFFF) - all slide backgrounds
  Accent 1: HSBC Red (#DB0011) - key metrics, section underlines, numbered badges, primary chart bars, left-border card accents
  Accent 2: Dark Grey (#4A4A4A) - body copy, outline icon strokes, descriptions
  Surface: Light Grey (#F5F5F5) - card backgrounds, grouped content areas
  Secondary: Medium Grey (#767676) - supporting text, secondary labels
  Chart Secondary: Slate Grey (#9B9B9B) - non-highlighted chart bars
  Divider: Light Rule (#E0E0E0) - thin horizontal rules between sections

Visual Elements:
  - Section subheaders: bold black text with a short (~40–60px) HSBC Red horizontal rule directly beneath, used to label every content section
  - Numbered badges: circular badges, two variants — outlined dark grey circle with white fill and dark grey number for sequential items, or filled HSBC Red circle with white number for priority items
  - Data callouts: large bold HSBC Red numbers (e.g. 72%, 10–15%, 60%) paired with regular dark grey labels, used to anchor key metrics
  - Red left-border cards: light grey (#F5F5F5) rectangular cards with a 4–6px bold HSBC Red left border, used to highlight key statements
  - Outline icons: thin-stroke outline icons in dark grey, never filled, placed above or beside labels, often with a HSBC Red horizontal rule below
  - Horizontal rule dividers: thin light grey rules separating rows of content within a column
  - Charts: bar charts with HSBC Red primary bar and Slate Grey comparison bar; donut charts with HSBC Red primary segment and dark grey secondary
  - Top navigation tab bar (optional): medium grey tabs with active tab in bold black underlined in HSBC Red, separated from content by full-width thin horizontal rule

Density Guidelines:
  - Content per slide: balanced — 1 main headline, 1 section subheader, 3–5 supporting points or metric rows; never crowd a slide
  - Whitespace: generous between all elements; if a slide feels full, split it into two

Style Rules:
  Do: Use HSBC Red exclusively for key numbers, section underlines, numbered badges, primary chart bars, and left-border accents. Apply section subheaders with a short red underline rule. Use thin-stroke outline icons consistently. Maintain generous whitespace. Use sentence case for titles. Group related content in light grey cards without heavy borders. Place footnotes at the very bottom in small light grey text.
  Don't: Use any background color other than white or light grey. Apply filled or coloured blocks as section headers. Use more than one accent colour beyond HSBC Red. Add drop shadows, gradients, or decorative effects. Use bold or filled icons. Mix font families. Crowd content.
</STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS>
