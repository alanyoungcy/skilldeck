## STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS

<STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS>
Design Aesthetic: Soft hand-drawn illustration style with relaxed brush strokes and warm, approachable character. Fresh, refined minimalist editorial aesthetic that emphasizes precision and clarity while staying friendly and human. Every element should feel sketched by hand, never mechanical or photorealistic.

Background:
  Texture: Warm off-white paper with subtle paper grain; slightly warm tone to avoid clinical feel; preserve hand-painted casual feel
  Base Color: Warm Off-White (#FAF8F0)

Typography:
  Headlines: Bold hand-written marker or cartoon poster lettering with slightly uneven baseline, thick strokes, soft edges — rendered as hand-drawn letters, not typed text
  Body: Clear handwritten round or hard-pen style mimicking everyday notes; consistent sizing with slight natural variation; legible casual handwriting

Color Palette:
  Primary Text: Deep Charcoal (#2C3E50) - Headlines and primary body text
  Background: Warm Off-White (#FAF8F0) - Primary background across all slides
  Accent 1: Soft Orange (#F4A261) - Highlights, key emphasis, callouts
  Accent 2: Mustard Yellow (#E9C46A) - Secondary highlights, decorative fills
  Accent 3: Sage Green (#87A96B) - Growth, adoption, positive outcomes
  Accent 4: Light Blue (#7EC8E3) - Process, methodology, structure
  Accent 5: Red Brown (#A0522D) - Foundational, structural elements
  Alt Text: Deep Brown (#4A4A4A) - Secondary text, captions

Visual Elements:
  - Hand-drawn wavy connection lines (never perfectly straight) linking concepts
  - Conceptual abstract icons (lightbulbs, arrows, gears, people figures) illustrating ideas, not literal scenes
  - Color fills that don't fully fill outlines — preserve casual hand-painted feel
  - Simple rounded-corner geometric shapes used as zones or containers
  - Sketchy arrows and pointers with informal hand-drawn quality
  - Doodle decorations: small stars, spirals, dots, hand-drawn underlines, asterisks
  - Subtle overlapping layers for depth

Density Guidelines:
  - Content per slide: 3-5 key elements; one clear headline plus supporting points or visual
  - Whitespace: Generous breathing room around all elements; never crowd the canvas
  - Open, well-structured layouts that emphasize hierarchy and readability

Style Rules:
  Do: Use hand-drawn quality on every element; allow slight wobbles and imperfection for character; layer with subtle overlaps; emphasize information hierarchy; keep the warm paper feeling visible
  Don't: Use perfect geometric shapes; create photorealistic elements; overcrowd the slide; use pure white backgrounds; add slide numbers, footers, or logos; use typed-looking text
</STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS>
