## STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS

<STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS>
Design Aesthetic: Polished enterprise consulting slides where structure does the persuading — crisp geometry, generous whitespace, modular grids, and strong typographic hierarchy carry every page. Color is surgical: deep corporate navy and IBM Blue establish authority while a single coral accent lands the one number that must be remembered. Charts are flat and unembellished; the only ornament is the rigor of the layout itself.

Background:
  Texture: Flat matte white on content slides, no gradients or noise; deep navy fills on title and divider slides; a thin 3–4 px IBM Blue accent rule across the top of each content slide
  Base Color: Pure White (#FFFFFF) for body slides, Deep Navy (#0F2A4A) for cover and section dividers

Typography:
  Headlines: Modern geometric humanist sans-serif at weight 600, sentence case, tight tracking, set as full-sentence findings rather than topic labels — clean rectangular letterforms with even stroke weight and a single-story 'a'
  Body: Same family at weight 400 for paragraphs and weight 500 for sub-headers, line height 1.4–1.5, dark slate ink, left-aligned; numerical hero stats rendered in a very light weight (200–300) at 60–96 pt with the qualifier sitting small and quiet beside or below

Color Palette:
  Primary Text: Slate (#262626) - body copy and axis labels
  Background: Pure White (#FFFFFF) - body slide canvas
  Accent 1: IBM Blue (#0F62FE) - headers, primary chart series, top accent rule, callouts
  Accent 2: Coral Red (#DA1E28) - the one critical number or insight per slide
  Section Background: Deep Navy (#0F2A4A) - cover, section dividers, hero panels
  Supporting: Steel Blue (#4589FF) - secondary chart series
  Tint: Pale Blue (#D0E2FF) - block fills, highlight zones, table header tints
  Cool Accent: Teal (#007D79) - tertiary series, positive outcome callouts
  Secondary Text: Mid Gray (#6F6F6F) - subtitles, source notes
  Divider: Light Gray (#E0E0E0) - 1 px rules, table grid lines

Visual Elements:
  - Modular column grids and thin 1 px dividers — every element snaps to baseline and column boundaries
  - Flat 2D charts (horizontal/vertical bars, donuts max 4 slices, simple line) with inline data labels and a single coral annotation arrow on the key data point
  - Hero stat blocks: oversized light-weight numeral, short slate rule beneath, one-line qualifier, 10 pt mid-gray source line
  - Process journeys rendered as equal-width rectangles with thin IBM Blue arrow connectors and small "01 / 02 / 03" stage numerals in pale blue
  - Comparison tables with pale blue header tint, alternating white / very pale gray rows, and a thin coral left-border on the "winning" column
  - Source line in mid-gray 9–10 pt at the bottom-left of every chart, table, or quoted statistic

Density Guidelines:
  - Content per slide: One finding headline + 2–4 supporting points OR one chart OR one diagram — never both a paragraph and bullets in the same zone
  - Whitespace: Generous side and top margins, ~25–35% of slide reserved as breathing room; one accent color per slide, one critical number per slide

Style Rules:
  Do: Write headlines as findings not topics; group information into 3s or 4s; reserve coral for ONE element per slide; pass the skim test (headlines alone tell the story); include a source line on every statistic; round numbers in callouts; align every element to the underlying grid; use sentence case throughout
  Don't: Use gradients, drop shadows, bevels, glows, or 3D effects; mix more than two typefaces; apply more than one accent color per slide; center-align body copy or bullets; use clipart, emoji, hand-drawn elements, or photo backgrounds behind text; crowd a slide — split into two if it doesn't fit calmly; use rainbow categorical palettes
</STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS>
