## STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS

<STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS>
Design Aesthetic: Polished enterprise consulting slides where structure does the persuading. Crisp geometry, generous whitespace, modular grids, and strong typographic hierarchy carry the slide. Color is surgical — a deep corporate blue for authority, a single coral accent for the one number per slide that must land. Charts are flat and unembellished; every visual proves a stated finding.

Background:
  Texture: Flat, matte, no gradients on body slides. Pure white content background with an optional thin (3–4 px) IBM Blue accent rule at the top. Title and divider slides use deep navy.
  Base Color: Pure White (#FFFFFF) for body slides; Deep Navy (#0F2A4A) for title/dividers.

Typography:
  Headlines: Clean geometric humanist sans-serif at weight 600, sentence case, written as findings (6–14 words). Tight letterspacing, generous line height. Numerical hero stats use an ultra-light (200–300) weight at 60–144 pt for dramatic contrast.
  Body: Same sans-serif family at weight 400 for body, weight 500 for sub-headers and labels. Slate text, line height 1.4–1.5. Sparse — one short paragraph OR 3–4 short bullets per zone, never both.

Color Palette:
  Primary Text: Slate (#262626) - Body copy, axis labels, table content
  Background: Pure White (#FFFFFF) - Body slide background
  Section Background: Deep Navy (#0F2A4A) - Title slides, dividers, hero panels
  Accent 1: IBM Blue (#0F62FE) - Headers, primary chart series, accent rules, callouts
  Accent 2: Coral Red (#DA1E28) - The one critical number / insight per slide
  Supporting: Steel Blue (#4589FF) - Secondary chart series
  Tint: Pale Blue (#D0E2FF) - Block fills, highlight zones, table tints
  Cool Accent: Teal (#007D79) - Tertiary series, positive outcome callouts
  Secondary Text: Mid Gray (#6F6F6F) - Subtitles, source notes
  Divider: Light Gray (#E0E0E0) - 1 px rules, table grid lines

Visual Elements:
  - Thin 3–4 px IBM Blue accent rule at the top edge of every content slide
  - Flat horizontal/vertical bar charts, donut charts (≤4 slices), simple line charts (≤3 series) — no shadows, no 3D, no gradients
  - Hero stat blocks: oversized ultra-light coral numerals with a 40 px slate rule beneath and a short qualifier sentence
  - Comparison tables with pale blue header tints, alternating row shading, and a thin coral left-border on the "winning" column
  - Small filled circle markers and 1.5 px IBM Blue connector arrows for process journeys
  - Source attribution line in mid-gray 9–10 pt at bottom-left of every chart and quoted stat
  - Slide numbers and section markers in pale blue, weight 500, top-left or bottom-right

Density Guidelines:
  - Content per slide: One finding headline + 2–4 supporting elements (stats, bullets, or one chart). Never both bullets and a paragraph in the same zone.
  - Whitespace: Generous — at least 8% margin on all sides. Elements align to a consistent 12-column grid; left edges, baselines, and column boundaries all line up.

Style Rules:
  Do: Write headlines as findings, not topics. Group information into 3s (4 max). Reserve coral for ONE element per slide. Pass the "skim test" — headline-only must tell the story. Round numbers in callouts. Use sentence case everywhere. Left-align body copy.
  Don't: Use gradients, drop shadows, bevels, glows, or 3D effects. Mix more than two typefaces. Apply more than one accent color per slide. Build slides without a finding headline. Crowd a slide. Use clipart, emoji, hand-drawn elements, or photo backgrounds behind text. Center-align body copy or bullets.
</STYLE_INSTRUCTIONS>
